USC...?

usc definitely has a reputation, whether it's good or bad is up to personal discretion.

i personally am completely in love with this school. i love the school spirit, the "work hard play hard" culture, the classes offered, the extracurricular clubs i'm a part of...the list goes on. but just because i like usc doesn't mean that you will too. hopefully you've all done your school research before you applied; if you didn't, i strongly recommend you to at least read about usc a bit before you come.

usc's reputation as a party school might be concerning to a lot of incoming students, especially with the school's recent scandals with the fraternities. some tips for navigating the school's party cultures:

  1. never go to a party alone, always have a trusted friend who will be able to take care of you
    • it's understandable that you and your friends will want to have fun at parties, but it's more important to be careful of your surroundings. one of you must stay sober; one of you must be the "mom" of the group.
  2. know the reputations of the parties you're going to
    • talk to your friends to see if they're well aquainted with the party scene. if they are, ask them about the parties you're going to. some fraternities are known for spiking their drinks. knowing what you're going into will help you prepare for what's to come.
  3. make sure to know your limit
    • know when to stop when it comes to substances. do not drink or smoke more than you can take. while you're looking to have fun on a night out, it's also imparative that you take care of yourself.
  4. remember to have fun!
    • studying and working hard all week (or even longer!) can be incredibly draining. have fun with your friends on a casual night out will definitely help release all your pent up stress! just remember to be careful while you're enjoying yourself!

after my first year at usc, i feel incredibly comfortable expressing my identities fully. everyone is inclusive, open minded, and just incredibly kind. i've met some of the kindest souls on earth here at this school. there are lot of oppotunities with extracurricular clubs and other events that really promote an inclusive learning environment.

dorm essentials

a dorm definitely won't be the same as your nice comfy room back home, but there are some things that can help!

  • mattress topper
  • nice comfortable sheets that fit your bed
  • pillows, blankets, and comforters
  • kettle
  • a brita filter
  • bath towels
  • clothes hangers
  • laundry basket
  • storage boxes
  • trash can
  • handheld vacuum (or a fullsize one)
  • utensil set
  • toolkit
  • desk lamp
  • power strip or surge protector

decoration tips


you'll probably want to decorate your dorm with cute lightings and posters hung up across your dorm room walls. they are very cute and will definitely make your dorm feel a lot more cozy, but something to keep in mind is your dorm's rules on hanging things on walls. for usc's dorms, you are not allowed to leave marks on walls, so definitely be careful if what you're planning to hang up will leave a mark on your walls.

while we all love a bit of christmas spirit in our dorm rooms, christmas trees and wreaths are also banned from usc dorms. there are cute alternatives to christmas trees being sold in the usc village target though!
